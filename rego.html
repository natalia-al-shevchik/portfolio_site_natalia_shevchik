ReGo Market: second-hand online store

Role: UX/UI Designer & Researcher · Duration: August-September 2025 · Tools: Figma, FigJam, Miro, Google Forms, Canva

ReGo Market is a thrift shopping app that makes it easy to browse second-hand items, save your favourites to a wishlist, and check out securely. The focus is on convenience, frugality, and sustainability, so the design is simple and streamlined.

Target audience

Eco-conscious shoppers, students and professionals who value sustainability and good deals. They prefer fast, clear mobile experiences that make secondhand shopping effortless.

Problem • Objective • Solution

Problem

Online thrifting often feels cluttered, unintuitive, and inconsistent — which creates friction and abandonment.

Objective

Create a smooth, familiar shopping experience while honoring the unique logic of second-hand items (single availability, variable sizing).

Solution

A streamlined app flow with wishlist flexibility, fast checkout, and a clean design system tested across breakpoints.

Usability testing — key findings

  • Toasts were expected at the top rather than the bottom — moved to top banners.
  • Added product info to the "added to cart" toast.
  • Order overview: include items, address, payment and totals before confirmation.
  • Refined input spacing and bottom nav behavior across devices.

Insights & learnings

  • Flow felt intuitive & straightforward to multiple testers
  • Wishlist → Cart → Checkout interaction worked naturally
  • Clear navigation (heart/share icons, browsing) felt familiar & expected
  • Design system iterations created a cohesive, consistent look
  • Toast notifications: visibility issue (expected at top, not bottom)
  • Order overview: missing details summary before confirmation caused friction
  • Spacing & alignment: input fields and nav icons felt off to testers
  • Refined feedback system (dialogues or banners instead of subtle toasts)
  • Animations & micro-interactions for smoother onboarding and checkout
  • Responsive refinements: test footer/nav placement across devices
